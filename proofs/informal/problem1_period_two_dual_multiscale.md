# Dual multiscale action of the period-two whole-word transducer

## Status

This note proves an all-depth structural theorem about the exact four-state
whole-word transducer used in the period-two reduction:

> The invertible dual action is self-replicating and transitive on every level
> of the rooted four-ary tree of scan-state words.

Consequently every dual boundary orbit realizes all `4^d` possible prefixes of
length `d` for every `d`.

This is a multiscale obstruction to a naive bounded recursive section state. It
does **not** prove that the alternating inverse lift has infinite support, does
not exclude eventual center period two, and does not solve Rule 30 center
nonperiodicity.

## 1. Whole-word transducer

Use the scan states

```text
Q = {00,01,10,11}
```

and the word alphabet

```text
A = {t,p,u}.
```

The exact transition table is:

| scan state | `t` | `p` | `u` |
|---|---|---|---|
| `00` | `t / 00` | `p / 11` | `p / 11` |
| `01` | `p / 01` | `u / 10` | `u / 10` |
| `10` | `p / 11` | `p / 01` | `t / 00` |
| `11` | `u / 10` | `t / 00` | `p / 01` |

Every column permutes the four scan states. The machine is therefore reversible
in its scan-state coordinate, and its dual acts by automorphisms on the rooted
tree `Q*`.

## 2. Wreath recursion of the dual

Write the dual generators using the same symbols `t,p,u`. The root
permutations on the ordered state alphabet `(00,01,10,11)` are

```text
sigma_t = (00)(01)(10 11)
sigma_p = (00 11)(01 10)
sigma_u = (00 11 01 10).
```

The sections are read from the emitted letters in the original table. Hence

```text
t = (t,p,p,u) sigma_t
p = (p,u,p,t) sigma_p
u = (p,u,t,p) sigma_u.
```

In particular, `sigma_u` is a four-cycle, so the dual action is transitive on
the first level.

## 3. Exact self-replication witnesses

Let uppercase letters denote inverses in the dual group. Direct wreath-recursion
multiplication gives:

```text
t     = (t,p,p,u) sigma_t
ppT   = (p,u,p,t) sigma_t
tPttP = (u,p,t,p) sigma_t.
```

All three root permutations fix the vertex `00`. Their sections at `00` are,
respectively,

```text
t, p, u.
```

Therefore the section homomorphism

```text
Stab(00) -> G
```

is onto, where `G=<t,p,u>` is the dual group.

Because the root action is transitive, conjugating these witnesses gives
surjective section maps at every first-level vertex.

## 4. Level-transitivity lemma

We use the following standard self-similar-group lemma, whose short proof is
included.

**Lemma.** Suppose a self-similar group `G` acts transitively on the first level
of `X*`, and for one first-level vertex `x` the section map
`Stab_G(x)->G` is onto. Then `G` acts transitively on `X^d` for every `d>=1`.

**Proof.** The claim is true for `d=1`. Assume transitivity on level `d`. Take
words `av` and `bw` of length `d+1`, with `a,b in X`. First choose `g in G`
with `g(a)=b`. Its section at `a` sends `v` to some word `v'` of length `d`.
By conjugating the surjective section map to the vertex `b`, choose
`h in Stab_G(b)` whose section at `b` sends `v'` to `w`; the induction
hypothesis supplies the required section element. Then `hg(av)=bw`. ∎

The hypotheses hold by Sections 2 and 3. Thus:

```text
G acts transitively on Q^d for every d>=1.
```

## 5. Boundary consequence

Let `omega in Q^omega` be any infinite scan-state word. For every depth `d` and
every prefix `v in Q^d`, level transitivity supplies `g in G` sending the
length-`d` prefix of `omega` to `v`. Therefore the orbit `G omega` has all
`4^d` possible prefixes at depth `d`.

So every dual boundary orbit is infinite and has maximal prefix complexity.
This conclusion is exact and all-depth.

This is consistent with the general dual-boundary approach to automaton
(semi)group freeness, but this note does not invoke a freeness theorem and does
not claim that the original scan-state semigroup is free.

## 6. Finite pair-orbit diagnostic

The analyzer also exhausts the diagonal dual action on ordered pairs of
distinct words through depth four. It finds exactly

```text
2d
```

ordered-pair orbits at depth `d`, with sizes:

```text
d=1: 4, 8
d=2: 16, 32, 64, 128
d=3: 64, 128, 256, 512, 1024, 2048
d=4: 256, 512, 1024, 2048, 4096, 8192, 16384, 32768.
```

Every checked orbit contains a pair with different root output signatures.
This is strong evidence for a simple all-depth pair-orbit law and for freeness
of equal-length state products, but neither statement is claimed here without
an induction proof.

## 7. Actual period-two word orbit

Let `J_m` be the actual low-to-high inverse word generated by

```text
J_(m+1) = q_m p tau_11(J_m),
```

with `q_m` supplied by the exact autonomous fringe orbit. For a word `J`, let
`S_d(J)` be the set of all words obtained by applying every sequence of `d`
scan states.

The exact finite campaign gives:

| block | `|J_m|` | section-orbit sizes for depths 1 through 8 |
|---:|---:|---|
| 4 | 8 | `4,16,62,200,394,491,514,517` |
| 8 | 16 | `4,16,64,256,1003,3695,11426,26043` |
| 16 | 32 | `4,16,64,256,1024,4096,16378,65413` |
| 32 | 64 | `4,16,64,256,1024,4096,16384,65536` |

Thus `J_32` realizes the maximum possible `4^d` distinct iterated sections for
every `d<=8`.

This is finite evidence only. It does not establish maximal section growth at
all depths.

## 8. Consequence for the research path

The natural recursive idea was to store the exact tower of transducer sections
and hope it closed to a small dyadic state. The theorem and diagnostics show
the opposite behavior:

1. the universal dual action reaches every state word at every scale;
2. every boundary orbit has maximal prefix complexity;
3. the actual period-two word already realizes maximal iterated-section growth
   through depth eight.

Therefore the unquotiented section tower is not a useful compact invariant. A
successful next observable must deliberately discard most universal dual
information while retaining something special to the unique actual fringe
orbit. The most plausible remaining directions are:

- a multi-time identity linking two or more moving seams;
- a quotient tied to the zero-initialized fringe orbit rather than every word;
- or a nonlocal order/valuation statistic on the original binary Rule 30 row.
