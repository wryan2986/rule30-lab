# Finite-depth universality of both period-two zero phases

Status: complete proof that every finite future branch driver is compatible with
a terminal-zero prefix in each of the two fixed phases. This is a finite-depth
no-go theorem. It does not construct one ordinary finite word surviving an
infinite driver, exclude eventual center period two, or solve Rule 30 center
nonperiodicity.

## 1. Context

Let

```text
Q = {00,01,10,11}
```

be the pair-state alphabet of the exact whole-word transducer. For an inverse
word `G`, its dual action on a state word in `Q^L` is denoted `tau_G`.

The preceding dual-cut theorem associates to a future branch driver

```text
q_0,...,q_(L-2)
```

a boundary word

```text
V_L(q) in Q^L.
```

For an initial accumulated word `G`, the next `L` terminal pairs are exactly

```text
tau_G(V_L(q)).
```

Thus the next `L` pairs are all `00` exactly when

```text
tau_G(V_L(q)) = 00^L.                              (1)
```

The fixed-phase theorem further says that a terminal-zero word has normalized
phase `p` or `u`, and that this phase remains constant throughout every
consecutive zero island.

A tempting continuation would be to search for a finite branch word, finite
dual depth, or finite phase cylinder forbidden in one of the two fibers. The
theorem below rules out that entire class.

## 2. Positive level transitivity

The dual multiscale theorem already proves that the group generated by
`t,p,u` acts transitively on `Q^L` for every `L>=1`.

At a fixed depth `L`, each generator acts as a permutation of the finite set
`Q^L`. Let `o_g(L)` be its finite permutation order. Then

```text
g^(-1) = g^(o_g(L)-1)
```

on `Q^L`. Every word involving inverse generators can therefore be replaced,
at that depth, by a positive word over `{t,p,u}`.

Consequently:

> **Positive level-transitivity lemma.** For every `v,w in Q^L`, there is a
> positive inverse-generator word `W` such that `tau_W(v)=w`.

In particular, for every future boundary word `V_L(q)`, there is a positive
word `W` with

```text
tau_W(V_L(q)) = 00^L.                              (2)
```

## 3. Phase padding

Fix a requested phase

```text
a in {p,u}.
```

Let `o_a(L)` be the order of the dual action of the single generator `a` on
`Q^L`. The positive word

```text
I_(a,L) = a^(o_a(L))
```

acts identically on the entire level:

```text
tau_(I_(a,L))(v)=v  for every v in Q^L.             (3)
```

Given a witness `W` from (2), define

```text
G_(a,L,q) = I_(a,L) W.                              (4)
```

The word in (4) begins with `a`, so it has normalized phase `a`; there is no
leading `t` to remove. Equations (2) and (3) give

```text
tau_(G_(a,L,q))(V_L(q)) = 00^L.                    (5)
```

The dual-cut theorem converts (5) into a complete terminal-zero prefix.
Because the first terminal pair is zero and the normalized word starts with
`a`, the fixed-phase theorem applies and the phase remains `a` at every cut
inside that prefix.

This proves the main theorem.

## 4. Finite-phase universality theorem

> **Theorem.** Fix any depth `L>=1`, any future driver
> `q_0,...,q_(L-2)`, and either phase `a in {p,u}`. There exists a finite
> accumulated inverse word `G_(a,L,q)` of normalized phase `a` whose next `L`
> terminal pairs under that driver are all `00`. The complete zero prefix stays
> in phase `a`.

Equivalently:

- every finite branch driver is compatible with the `p/11` fiber;
- every finite branch driver is compatible with the `u/10` fiber;
- every finite dual-depth zero target has witnesses in both phases.

No finite forbidden branch word can distinguish the two fibers or exclude an
infinite final zero tail.

## 5. Why this does not construct a counterexample

The witness in (4) depends on the requested depth `L`. Its phase padding uses
the finite-level order `o_a(L)`, and the transitivity witness `W` may also grow
with `L`.

The theorem therefore gives a family

```text
G_(a,1,q), G_(a,2,q), ...
```

of finite shadows. It does not give one fixed ordinary word that works at all
depths. A compatible inverse-limit boundary point can have infinite 2-adic
support, exactly as in the earlier schedule-survivor construction.

Hence finite-depth compactness, finite branch-language exclusions, and fixed
phase cylinders cannot settle the support problem.

## 7. Research consequence

The fixed-phase reduction remains exact and useful, but it cannot be completed
by any obstruction visible at one finite schedule depth. The next successful
argument must compare the entire nested sequence of phase witnesses and prove
that its unique inverse-limit point is not an ordinary finite word.

Admissible directions include:

1. a growth lower bound for the minimal phase-padded witness as depth increases;
2. an incompatibility between successive minimal witnesses under truncation;
3. a nonlocal invariant of the infinite dual boundary orbit;
4. an original-spacetime theorem forcing infinitely many finite-depth
   past/future mismatches on the actual zero-initialized fringe orbit.

## 8. Scientific boundary

This result does not prove that the alternating inverse lift has infinite
support, exclude eventual center period two, or solve Rule 30 center
nonperiodicity. It proves that both fixed phases possess complete finite-depth
branch freedom, so the remaining obstruction is necessarily infinite-scale.
